We planned to visit Shirakawa during our December/January trip to Japan, ever since I cam across a Bing wallpaper featuring the village.

However, the day before we were heading to Takayama, we ran into a setback: all the reservable day-trip buses were fully booked. We were disappointed, knowing that winter is the only time we could see the village covered in snow, and we regretted not booking the tickets earlier.

Still, we stuck to our itinerary. Two days later, we headed to the bus stop early, hoping to secure seats on one of the few non-reservable buses. To our amazement, a staff member told us that extra buses had been added due to the crowds - and just like that, we were able to board the very first one departing!

When we arrived, it felt like we were stepping into a winter wonderland.  The thatched-roof farmhouses were covered in snow, and from the panoramic viewpoint above, the scene was breathtaking. 

We wandered through the village, visiting a few farmhouses and stores.  Yes, it’s a touristy destination, but it was such a magical experience.  We were truly glad that we left the comfort of our cozy ryokan to make the trip - it ended up being one the highlights of our Japan trip.
