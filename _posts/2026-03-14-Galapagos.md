The Galápagos Islands had long captured my imagination. I first became fascinated with the place after watching Master and Commander and doing a school project about Charles Darwin. At the time, I didn’t even realize it was possible to visit the islands. That changed when some friends from university traveled there during one summer break. From then on, the idea of going there took hold of me—the thought of seeing giant tortoises, Darwin’s finches, and other unusual wildlife.

Many years later, I finally decided to make the trip, and my sister agreed to join me. There was just one catch: neither of us wanted to swim. 

When we arrived on the main island, Santa Cruz, the first thing we did was visit a tour agency. We told the friendly agent that we were open to any tour as long as it didn’t involve swimming. After listening to our request, he suggested a trip to Floreana Island, explaining that it included walking around the island and that we would still be able to see wildlife without snorkeling.

The next day we joined our group tour at the dock and quickly announced to the guide and the group, “Just to let you know, we’re not swimming.” Everyone’s faces dropped. They looked at us in disbelief. The guide then urged us to change our mind. He explained that we could wear full wetsuits, that he could teach us how to snorkel, and that we won’t be able to see many of the animals if we stayed out of the water. That last argument made us hesitate.

![Floreana Island]({{ "" | relative_url }}/assets/images/Galapagos1.jpg)

In the end, we decided to give it a try and it turned out to be one of the best decisions of the trip. Snorkeling was easier and more fun than we had imagined, and the experience was unforgettable. Seeing the tortoises and other marine life underwater was incredible. My sister even had a memorable moment when a curious sea lion spiraled down through the water and came face to face with her.

A few days later, we booked another snorkeling trip on Isabela Island which was another wonderful experience.

Looking back, I’m so glad we made the trip and that we went snorkeling. Even now, thinking about the Galápagos always makes me smile.
