It had long been my dream to visit Miyajima and see the famous “temple in the water.” My sister and I went there for a short stay, and I felt so happy when we first approached the island and saw the iconic red torii gate.

![Miyajima’s famous torii gate]({{ "" | relative_url }}/assets/images/Miyajima1.jpg){: .align-left}

Later, we were surprised to come across a piece of paper mentioning that a special festival would be taking place soon. We had no idea it was happening and it was by chance that we were in Miyajima around that time.

Sadly, my sister was too tired to attend the first ceremony. It was a procession where priests from Itsukushima Shrine walked beneath the torii gate at low tide. I wish she had been able to see it.

![Festival]({{ "" | relative_url }}/assets/images/Miyajima2.jpg)

That evening, however, we were both able to attend another part of the festival. We watched traditional, brightly colored boats carrying musicians across the bay from the shrine. Dressed in ancient court robes, they played classical court music late into the night, illuminated by lantern light.
